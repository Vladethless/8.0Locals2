Metal roofing

HomeMetal roofing

Do you live in areas with extreme weather conditions? Is snow, heavy rainfall, or rain common where you live? On the flip side, is extreme heat more common where you live? Well, if you relate to any of these conditions, you can likely agree that the importance of using the right kind of materials to provide shelter can make all the difference in the world for you.

Whether it's protection from the adverse effects or the weather, resistivity to them, insulation of the house, and durability, why settle for anything that provides more minor than these baseline requirements.

You'll surely know how important it is for your housing materials, and more specifically, your ceiling materials, to fulfill all these conditions to make your life easy and comfortable.

However, some might say finding all these qualities in one ceiling and to the degree you need is impossible; compromise is required. We say there is no need to compromise when building your dream home.

This is why we recommend metal roofing for our clients looking for roofs with well-rounded properties and go the extra mile to ensure that your house is protected.

Why do you ask? Well, look at a few of the pros of using these roofs:

Durability

Metal is one of the most robust roofing materials out there. It can easily withstand any damage the environment can inflict on your house, whether it's more gradual or immediate.

From winds as fast as 140 miles per hour to the blistering hot heat to hail, your metal roof can take it all without corroding or cracking, thanks to its impact resistivity.

In addition, it is also fire resistant and will not ignite in case of a lightning strike or wildfire in the area.

You will not have to worry about regularly repairing or upkeeping the ceiling because it is a challenging and resistant material built to last.

Longevity

Speaking of being built to last, metal roofing has an average life span of 40 to 70 years, which means that at least in this lifetime, you won't have to worry about re-roof your house or constantly paying for roof replacement services.

Energy Efficiency

Metal roofing is known for its efficiency in reflecting the heat and UV rays of the sun. This means it can help maintain the internal temperature and provide an insulated environment that is not as quickly affected by external atmospheric conditions.

In other words, they can heat or cool your house and save you on energy costs. You will immediately notice a 20 to 25% reduction in your cooling costs after the installation of your metal roofing in [location].

Environmentally Beneficial

Another thing to note about these roofs is that they are made of 20-25% recycled material, and at the end of their lifespan, when they are discarded, they can 100% be recycled as well, meaning you do not have to worry about waste and pollution.
